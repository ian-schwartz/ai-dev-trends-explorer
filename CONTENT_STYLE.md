# Content Style Guide

Agents modifying content should follow CONTENT_STYLE.md when writing or updating descriptions.

---

## 1. Clarity and simplicity

- Prefer clear, simple sentences.
- Avoid overly complex punctuation.

## 2. Semicolons

- Avoid semicolons.
- Use periods or commas instead.

## 3. Colons

- Avoid excessive colons.
- Only use them when absolutely necessary for lists.

## 4. Dashes

- Avoid overusing dashes.
- Use normal sentence structure instead.

## 5. Paragraphs and phrasing

- Prefer short paragraphs and direct phrasing.

## 6. Tone

- Write in a neutral, informative tone.
- The site should feel like a technical field guide, not marketing copy.

## 7. Hype language

Avoid hype language such as:

- revolutionary
- groundbreaking
- game-changing

## 8. Practical descriptions

Favor practical descriptions that explain:

- what the workflow or tool does
- when developers should use it

## 9. Casing

- Maintain consistent casing for titles and headings.

## 10. Length

- Keep descriptions concise.
- One or two sentences is usually enough.
